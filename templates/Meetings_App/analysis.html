This page presents analytical insights derived from the event monitoring database. Each section below corresponds to a complex SQL query that combines multiple conditions across events, participants, and observation records in order to uncover meaningful patterns.

Participants with Repeated Exposure to Busy Meetings

This analysis identifies participants who frequently appear in high-density events and examines their exposure to risk.

A busy meeting is defined as a meeting with at least 5 participants, where at least one additional meeting took place in the same location on the same date.

The query focuses on participants who are consistently observed in low-risk situations, meaning that in every meeting where they did not report risk, there exists at least one observation record linked to them.

For each participant who attended at least 5 busy meetings, the table shows:

  • Participant ID – unique identifier of the participant
  • Busy Meeting Count – number of busy meetings attended
  • Risky Busy Meetings – number of those meetings where the participant reported risk

Why it matters: This helps identify participants who are frequently exposed to high-density environments and compare their actual risk perception with their behavioral patterns.

Critical Events and Highest Confidence Observers

This analysis identifies events with intensive and reliable monitoring activity.

A critical event is defined as a meeting where:

  • at least 3 different observers performed monitoring
  • no observation has a confidence score below 60

The query also introduces broad-coverage observers, defined as observers who have performed monitoring in every location where meetings take place.

The analysis selects critical events where at least two observations were performed by such broad-coverage observers.

For each selected event, the table shows the observer(s) who reported the highest confidence score during that meeting.

Why it matters: This highlights events with unusually strong and reliable monitoring activity, helping detect situations where multiple observers consistently report high confidence.

Efficient Monitoring Outside Preferred Locations

This analysis evaluates observer performance under non-ideal conditions.

A missed observation occurs when:

  • a participant reported risk during a meeting
  • the observer’s confidence score is lower than their own average for the same observation method

An efficient observer in a meeting is one who:

  • monitored at least 2 participants
  • had no missed observations in that meeting

A non-preferred meeting is a meeting where all observations were performed outside the observer’s preferred location.

For each location, the table shows how many such meetings occurred where at least one efficient observer was present.

Why it matters: This helps evaluate observer effectiveness in non-preferred environments, revealing whether strong performance is maintained outside familiar locations.
